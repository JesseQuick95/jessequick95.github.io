Computational Fluid Dynamics

CFD is an effective tool for analysing complex flows for industrial applications. CFD can be used to predict system performance, replacing expensive and time-consuming tests.

By specializing in CFD modelling I have undertaken many projects in this area. My projects consist of computationally cheap 2D Reynolds Averaged Navier-Stokes (RANS) simulations to highly accurate but expensive 3D Large Eddy Simulations (LES).

Swirling impinging jet cooling for a novel solar receiver design

In my Masters thesis I analysed complex flows for swirling impinging jets. This project exposed me to 2D axi-symmetric flows, 3D RANS simulations and LES. As can be seen in the following image the flow behaviour is highly complex but can be used for effective cooling in a multitude of applications.

A video of the flow simulation is found in the YouTube link under social or by clicking here.

Jet impingement cooling is used in industrial applications for CPU cooling and cooling turbine blade tips. This is due to the high heat transfer rates that can be achieved of up to a 3X increase compared to a pipe flow configuration.

The simulation described was the first simulation of its kind with very little experimental work done. The applications of this novel cooling method has shown enormous promise and in my study I found an improvement of 40% compared to conventional jet impingement cooling.
